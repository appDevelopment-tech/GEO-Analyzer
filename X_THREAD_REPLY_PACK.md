# X Thread Reply Pack (GEO/AEO/SEO)

Ready-to-post replies based on the latest discussion thread.

## 1) Why are ChatGPT numbers different from Claude/Perplexity?
Different systems, different retrieval behavior, different citation UI.  
I treat them as separate channels with separate baselines, then compare direction over time, not raw counts.

## 2) Is it just market share?
Market share matters for impact, but measurement differences are also technical: crawler access, rendering, query intent handling, and source selection logic.

## 3) What is SSR?
SSR = server-side rendering.  
The server returns usable HTML immediately, so crawlers (and users) can parse critical content without waiting for heavy client-side hydration.

## 4) Why can content be strong but citations still low?
Usually one of these: weak direct-answer formatting, thin evidence links, unclear entity signals, or inconsistent canonical/crawl setup.

## 5) Is patience enough?
Patience helps, but iteration beats patience.  
Run a 30-day loop: direct-answer rewrite, FAQ schema, cluster links, then measure weekly.

## 6) “4k citations with zero backlinks” sounds wild
It can happen in bursts, but I focus on repeatability over screenshots.  
If results cannot be reproduced with non-branded prompts, it is not yet a durable signal.

## 7) Distribution > backlinks?
For speed: often yes.  
For durability: best mix is distribution + strong source pages + selective authority links.

## 8) “How do I improve SEO for a new dev-marketing blog?”
Start with one tight topic cluster: 1 pillar page + 6 support pages.  
Each page gets a 40–60 word direct answer, evidence links, and clear internal linking.

## 9) What about Reddit/Digg discovery?
Useful for discovery velocity if the content is genuinely useful.  
Spammy distribution spikes impressions and kills trust quickly.

## 10) How do you avoid brand-bias in prompts?
Use three sets: non-branded category prompts, competitor comparison prompts, and branded prompts.  
Report them separately so results are interpretable.

## 11) “Backlinks are dead?”
Not dead, repriced.  
Links still matter, but poor structure + weak evidence can’t be rescued by outreach alone.

## 12) “Agents + search data outrank manual links?”
Instrumented feedback loops can outperform ad-hoc outreach, yes.  
But they work best when content quality and technical hygiene are already strong.
